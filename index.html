More about World Cup Flags Shop

For this project, I decided to make a column for each relevant section: filters, sorting, items, cart. I made these titles the biggest to indicate that there were 4 main sections. From there, the items column/section had flags with relevant details that were in a smaller font size to indicate a lower status. Finally, I used contrast within the add and remove button for each item. Lastly, I decided to frame the cart as one with the items and price along with a reset button.

For my application, I decided to use states to keep track of the type of items to be displayed, the cart of items, the price of the cart, and whether the items should be sorted. I also defined helpers such as: removeFromCart, updateCart, and a comparator function. Overall, I was able to get comfortable with the React framework.



More about Yhangry

Although the start-up, yhangry, had their own website, this design was not influnced by them. Instead, a series of brainstorms and sketches led to the design. Specifically, it was decided to start the home page with a button called "Book a Chef" that would take one to the Chef Selection page. Upon reaching this page, it would prompt one to fill out a filter criteria, then the appropriate chefs would appear. Lastly, a nav bar was added to help users navigate quickly from the home page, chef selection page, and bookings page.

In addition to the design process, the user testing was also a success. By using a third party site, I was able to see the strengths and flaws of our design. This helped us modify and perfect our app.

More about Wikipedia Redesign

In this Wikipedia redesign, problems that arose included: overwhelming amount of information, contents page in the middle made navigation difficult, a lack of alternative text for when images cannot be produced. Although many others have proposed a redesign for Wikipedia, the approach I took was making it accessible for those who may have problems with sight.

In the design, I decided to have a navbar at the top so that the user could explore the search function, language translation, and other options. Then, I added the contents page on the left side and had it fixed so that a user can navigate to any relevant section without having to scroll back up to navigate.

Overall, I learned how hard it is to redesign a website since you must account for all intended users. In general the tradeoff I made was prioritizing users who had issues with sight.
